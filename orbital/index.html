What is Orbital Resonance?
When two bodies maintain a simple integer ratio of orbital periods, they're in resonance. Gravitational nudges repeat at the same orbital positions, locking the bodies into a geometric relationship. Select a ratio — the trail of connecting lines reveals the pattern. Resonant ratios form closed, repeating figures; irrational ratios fill space uniformly.

Jupiter & Saturn's 5:2 Near-Resonance
Jupiter orbits in 11.86 years; Saturn in 29.46 years — a ratio of ~2.48, tantalizingly close to 5:2. Every ~19.9 years they meet in conjunction. The near-resonance clusters these meetings near three points roughly 120° apart, creating a slow gravitational drumbeat that reverberates through the solar system.

The Grand Tack
Jupiter formed beyond the frost line and began migrating inward — threatening the inner solar system. Saturn's arrival and a 3:2 resonance lock reversed both planets' migration, preserving conditions for Earth's formation.
